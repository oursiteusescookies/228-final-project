about

Welcome to my art gallery! I’m Anna, a cracked egg on the floor. I'm currently studying in the University of Waterloo's GBDA program.

Here, you’ll find creations from high school to university. Mediums range from paintings to 3D models to UI designs.

Please enjoy your visit and remember not to lick the artwork!

PHYSICAL ART

Behold, my physical collection!

Four of these were created in my last semester of grade 12, where I explored my chosen theme of Surrealism. I went about this by juxtaposing familiar settings with unexpected items and creating pieces that felt like windows to other worlds.

As for mediums, I'm most comfortable with markers, acrylic paint, and graphite. I definitely prefer traditional illustration over digital.

"Shark Attack"

Acrylic on 24" x 24" canvas, 2024

The first painting to start off my Surrealism series in grade 12. I really like the idea of sea animals swimming over land. You'll see this theme of misplacement continue in my other Surrealism works.

"Polluted"

Acrylic on 30" x 24" canvas, 2023

This is my biggest painting ever!!! It was pretty daunting at first, but after a while I got to appreciate how in-detail the canvas size enabled me to go. It's from grade 11, and still one of my proudest works to this day. I really don't know I pulled this off... I'd probably hurt myself trying to recreate it now.

"Ancient Bath"

Floral foam and resin in a bowl, 2024

An ancient Chinese servant bathes a royal Guanlong. This was the most ambitious project in my series. I'd been wanting to make a dinosaur diorama since I was a kid, so this was me living out that dream. It was my first time using resin, and took a month of basement and art class time to make.

"Jester"

Acrylic on 16" x 20" canvas, 2024

My last painting of high school. Rushed by deadlines, but still perfected by long days down in the basement. This came out edgier than intended, but I really wanted to go beyond my theme of misplacement and paint a juxtaposition no one's seen before.

"A Special Place"

Oil pastel on paper, 2024

This was one of my simpler illustrations. It served mainly as a quick breather during and after my resin bowl project, when I was waiting for the resin to dry. I was aiming for a sense of nostalgia and natural harmony in this work.

"Nausea"

Markers on 8.5" x 11" sketchbook paper, 2024

I relied on markers so much in high school. They're clean and don't require any tedious setup like painting, so they're perfect for quickly illustrating ideas or feelings late at night.

"Survival"

Acrylic on 8.5" x 11" sketchbook paper, 2023

One of my sketchbook prompts from grade 11. I was short on time and I definitely didn't need to go so in detail for this assignment, but I enjoyed the concept so much I just couldn't help myself and wanted to make it perfect.

"Cracked"

Acrylic on an 11.5" x 11.5" vinyl record, 2023

This is what I look like! It's from grade 11 so it's not part of my Surrealism series, but still an icon. It's on an old vinyl record, but unfortunately, you can't play it.

DIGITAL ART

My digital works!

I learned how to use Blender during my Design courses in high school. My favourite kind of art is creating little snapshots of imaginary worlds, elements that convey narratives.

"Chestshire Cat"

3D model on Blender, 2022

An ominous, ever-smiling cat genie with mouths for eyes! He lives attached to a chest in the woods and smites those who are mean to him. I wanted to convey hints of narrative with an unsettling character design.

"Mushroom Cottage"

3D model on Blender, 2022

One of my first ever Blender models! It's really old, made for an assignment all the way back in grade 10. It's such a humble cottage you'd imagine there's not mushroom in there, but perhaps you'd be surprised!

"Trail Cam"

Digital illustration on Procreate, 2025

Just another one of those ideas that you put off for months or years and impulsively decide to commit to it on a random night. This is the first university work featured in this gallery... I probably should have been doing schoolwork instead, but at least it's pretty.

"Popsicle"

Vector illustration on Adobe Illustrator, 2022

This was from an assignment in grade 10, where I traced over a childhood photograph with Illustrator shapes. I really like how this came out and I'm glad I went so in-detail, even if I was cursing at myself for it back then.

"Pocket Closet"

Interactive app prototype on Figma, 2025

The most recent and last addition in this gallery, a group project that's still ongoing at the time I'm writing this! We're expected to finish on December 8 2025. It's the UI design for a wardrobe curation app that you can build and generate outfits on. We started 4 months ago, from researching and ideation to now finalizing our high-fidelity working prototype. Try it out here!
