Hello! I’m Anna, a cracked egg on the floor from Alberta, Canada.
I'm currently in the GBDA program at the University of Waterloo.

Welcome to my art gallery!
Here, you’ll find creations from high school to university. You'll find all sorts of mediums here, from paintings to 3D models to UI designs.

Please enjoy your visit and remember not to lick the artwork!

PHYSICAL ART

Take a look at my physical collection!

4 out of 7 of these were created in my last 5 months of grade 12, where I was tasked with exploring my chosen theme of surrealism.

For me, painting is an hours-long trance where I can shut off my mind and let my brush strokes lead the way.

"Shark Attack"

Acrylic paint on canvas. The first painting of my Surrealism series.

"Polluted"

Acrylic paint on a massive 24" x 12" canvas. This was from grade 11, but still one of my proudest works to this day.

"Ancient Bath"

Floral foam and resin in a bowl. An ancient Chinese servant bathes a royal Guanlong. My most ambitious work of my Surrealism series. This was my first time using resin, and took a month of basement and art class time to make.

"Jester"

Acrylic paint on canvas. My last painting of high school and my series. Rushed by deadlines, but still perfected by long days in the basement.

"See Life Farmland"

Oil pastel on paper. You'll see a lot of my artworks contain animals and misplacement. One of my favourite visual concepts is sealife in places they shouldn't be. For instance, the sky.

"Nausea"

Markers on sketchbook paper. I used markers so much in high school. They're easy to use and don't require any setup like painting! Perfect for impulsively staying up late.

"Survival"

Acrylic paint on sketchbook paper. One of my sketchbook prompt assignments from grade 11. Completed not in the basement, but undoubtedly at a late hour with a podcast in my ears.

"Cracked"

Acrylic paint on a vinyl record. This is what I look like! It's from grade 11 so not part of my series, but still an icon.
